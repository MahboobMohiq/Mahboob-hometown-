Explore Herat

Herat, known as the jewel of the East, boasts a rich history that dates back thousands of years. The city is famous for its stunning architecture, vibrant bazaars, and the warmth of its people. From the iconic Herat Citadel to the serene Herat Mosque, every corner tells a story.

Historical Sites

Herat is home to numerous historical landmarks. The Herat Citadel, also known as the Citadel of Alexander, is a UNESCO World Heritage site that has stood for centuries. Other notable sites include the Friday Mosque, known for its intricate tile work, and the Mausoleum of Goharshad.

Culture

Did You Know?

Herat is famous for its traditional crafts, especially carpets and pottery, which are sought after both locally and internationally.

FAQ: What is the best time to visit?

The best time to visit Herat is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant.

What to taste?

Don't miss trying Herati cuisine, especially dishes like Qabuli Pulao and Sheer Korma, which reflect the region's rich culinary heritage.
